This isZensor

A mental health journaling app

In Spring 2022, I worked with a design team of 4 to explore the need of a mental health journaling app meant to be used by college students.

The formative phase of our research focused on exploring the key pain points college students face when dealing with various mental health issues such as stress, depression, and anxiety all while having to juggle academic responsibilities. To gather results, we conducted one-on-one interviews with college students ranging from age 18-22 and have either been self or clinically diagnosed with some type of mental health issue. After conducting preliminary interviews, the evaluative phase consisted of designing a hi-fidelity prototype of a mental health journaling app called Zensor which included the following key features retreived from our interviews:

  • Jot down thoughts and feelings throughout the day

  • Scheduling an appointment with a therapist

  • Listen to calming ambient music when overwhelmed

    • We were able to conduct usability studies with 5 college students and received generally positive feedback about the app's usability and feasibility. Most points of feedback revolved around placing more emphasis on quick solutions that users can actually rely on during stressful events such as panic attacks and smoothening out interactions with the scheduling interface. Overall, I learned a lot about Figma prototyping and planning out usability studies during this project. Please feel free to check out the prototype here!
