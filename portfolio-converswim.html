This is Converswim

A conversation reflection app

The Project

For my senior year capstone, I had the opportunity to work alongside Annuska Zolyomi to design an application called Converswim, a conversation reflection tool meant to be used by autistic adults. The motivation to develop this app came from prior research conducted by Zolyomi which explored the challenges that autistic adults face in reading emotion, body language, and other non-verbal social cues when it comes to conversating with neurotypical individuals.

What I did

Most of my capstone work was done in the design phase, in which I was able to conduct several usability studies with a paper-prototype that demonstrates initial screen designs and intended interactions of the application. Through an online flyer, I recruited paired participants in which one or both parties identified as having some functioning form of autism. During my research, the goal was to acquire data on the usability of the application, feature usage, and overall first impressions.

What I learned + Findings

My research team, consisting of Professor Zolyomi, Hiba Hasan, a Psychology student at UW Bothell, and I received insightful feedback on how to continually optimize the app to ensure that the application suits our target users’ needs. The following are the quantitative and qualitative results we found on key feature usage:

  • 75% of participants utilized “Create” feature
  • 75% of participants utilized “Text” feature
  • 50% of participants utilized “More Icons” button
  • Desired feature to add more “levels” to conversation board
  • Overall desire for a tutorial walkthrough for first time users

    • If Converswim were an actual company, I believe that my research would provide groundbreaking insight into how the usability, feasibility, and viability of the product can be improved especially in the early stages of its design. This project helped me gain insight into the technicalities of leading an entire usability study on my own, and just how important it is to keep key user personas in mind when designing interfaces.
